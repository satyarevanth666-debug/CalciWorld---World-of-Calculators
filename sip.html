SIP Calculator Guide

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) helps you invest a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds or other investment products. The SIP calculator estimates the future value of your monthly investments using a compound interest formula.

What this calculator does

The SIP calculator estimates how much your regular monthly contributions can grow to over time, based on an expected annual return rate.

Formula used

The future value of SIP is calculated as:

FV = P × ((1 + r)^n - 1) / r × (1 + r)

Where:

  • P = monthly investment amount
  • r = monthly return rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = number of monthly investments

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Enter the monthly investment amount.
  2. Enter the expected annual return percentage.
  3. Enter the investment duration in years.
  4. Click calculate to see the projected future value.

Worked examples

Example 1

Suppose you invest ₹5,000 per month for 10 years at 12% annual return.

Monthly rate = 0.12 ÷ 12 = 0.01

n = 10 × 12 = 120

The SIP calculator estimates a future value of approximately ₹14,56,000.

Example 2

Suppose you invest ₹10,000 per month for 15 years at 10% annual return.

This gives a future value of roughly ₹43,00,000, illustrating the power of long-term compounding.

Use cases

  • Plan long-term savings for retirement or education.
  • Compare different monthly investment amounts.
  • Estimate how much your savings can grow over time.
  • See the effect of higher or lower expected returns.

FAQs

Can I use this for any investment type?

The calculator shows estimates for regular monthly investments and assumed returns. It is useful for SIP-like investments, but actual returns may vary.

What is the difference between SIP and lump sum?

SIP invests regularly over time, which helps average out market fluctuations. A lump sum is a one-time investment.

Do returns change every year?

This calculator assumes a fixed annual return for modeling purposes. In reality, returns can change year to year.

How to use this page

Use the SIP calculator with your planned monthly contribution and expected return. If you're just starting, enter a conservative rate and compare different time horizons.
