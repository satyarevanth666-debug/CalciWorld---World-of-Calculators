Discount Calculator Guide

The discount calculator helps you figure out how much you save when a product is on sale. It is useful for shopping, budgeting, and comparing multiple offers.

What this calculator does

It calculates the discount amount and the final price after discount based on the original price and discount percentage.

Formula used

The basic formula is:

Discount = Original Price × Discount % ÷ 100
Final Price = Original Price − Discount

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Enter the original price of the item.
  2. Enter the discount percentage.
  3. Use the result to decide whether the sale is a good deal.

Example

Original price = ₹2,000, Discount = 25%

You save ₹500 and pay ₹1,500.

When to use it

  • Compare sales prices for electronics, clothing, and groceries.
  • Decide whether an offer is worth the cost.
  • Estimate your total shopping expense with multiple discounts.

Frequently asked questions

Does this include tax?

No. If tax applies, calculate the discounted price first and then add tax separately or use the shopping discount calculator for both values.

Can I use this on multiple items?

Yes, calculate each item's discounted price individually and sum the totals for the whole purchase.
