Loan Calculator Guide

Use the loan calculator to estimate monthly payments when borrowing money for a home, car, education, or personal expense. This guide explains how principal, interest rate, and repayment tenure interact.

What this calculator tells you

The loan calculator uses the standard amortization formula to compute the monthly payment required to repay the loan over a fixed number of months.

Formula used

The monthly payment is calculated as:

EMI = P × i × (1 + i)^n / ((1 + i)^n − 1)

Where:

  • P = principal loan amount
  • i = monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
  • n = total number of monthly payments

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Enter the loan amount you are borrowing.
  2. Enter the annual interest rate.
  3. Enter the repayment period in months.
  4. Calculate to see the monthly payment, total payment, and total interest.

Example

Loan = ₹500,000, Rate = 8% p.a., Tenure = 60 months.

Monthly EMI = ₹10,135 (approx). Total interest over 5 years is ₹108,100.

Practical tips

  • Compare loans by keeping the amount constant and changing the interest rate or tenure.
  • A shorter tenure lowers interest but raises the monthly payment.
  • A lower interest rate saves money over the life of the loan.

FAQs

What should I use as tenure?

Enter the total number of monthly payments. For example, 5 years is 60 months, 10 years is 120 months.

Does this calculator include fees?

It calculates interest using the loan amount only. Add extra fees separately to estimate your total cost.

Is EMI the same as total cost?

No. EMI is the monthly payment. Total cost includes the principal plus total interest paid over the loan term.
