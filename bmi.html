BMI Calculator Guide

The Body Mass Index (BMI) calculator helps you estimate whether your weight is within a healthy range for your height. It is a simple screening tool used by health professionals to identify potential weight-related health risks.

What this calculator does

Using your weight and height, the BMI calculator computes a numeric value that can be compared against standard categories like underweight, normal weight, overweight, and obesity.

Formula used

The BMI formula is:

BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m) × height (m))

If your height is in centimeters, first convert it to meters by dividing by 100.

Step-by-step instructions

  1. Enter your weight in kilograms.
  2. Enter your height in meters or convert your height from centimeters.
  3. Click calculate to see your BMI value.
  4. Compare your result with the standard BMI ranges below.

Standard BMI categories

  • Underweight: BMI less than 18.5
  • Normal weight: BMI 18.5–24.9
  • Overweight: BMI 25–29.9
  • Obesity: BMI 30 or higher

Worked examples

Example 1

Weight = 70 kg, Height = 1.75 m

BMI = 70 / (1.75 × 1.75) = 22.9

This value is within the normal weight range.

Example 2

Weight = 85 kg, Height = 1.68 m

BMI = 85 / (1.68 × 1.68) = 30.1

This value falls into the obesity category, which suggests you may want to consult a healthcare professional.

Use cases

  • Track your weight status over time.
  • Compare your BMI before and after a diet or exercise program.
  • Screen for potential health risks related to body weight.
  • Share your BMI with a doctor or coach to support health planning.

FAQs

Is BMI a perfect measure of health?

No. BMI is a simple screening tool and does not account for muscle mass, bone density, or fat distribution. Use it as one part of a broader health assessment.

Can children use this calculator?

Children and teens have different BMI standards, so use a pediatric growth chart or a calculator designed for age-adjusted BMI.

What if I prefer pounds and inches?

Convert pounds to kilograms by dividing by 2.2046, and convert inches to meters by multiplying by 0.0254 before using this calculator.

How to use this page

Use the BMI calculator with your current height and weight. Then use the examples above to compare your result and decide if you need to adjust your lifestyle or seek advice.
